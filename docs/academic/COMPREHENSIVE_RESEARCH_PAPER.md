## 3. Theoretical Methods

### 3.1 Bethe-Weizsäcker Semi-Empirical Mass Formula

The binding energy B(Z,N) is calculated as:

```
B(Z,N) = aᵥA - aₛA^(2/3) - aᶜZ(Z-1)/A^(1/3) - aₐ(N-Z)²/A + δ(A,Z)
```

**Coefficients (MeV):**
| Term | Symbol | Value | Physical Meaning |
|------|--------|-------|------------------|
| Volume | aᵥ | 15.75 | Bulk nuclear matter |
| Surface | aₛ | 17.80 | Surface tension |
| Coulomb | aᶜ | 0.711 | Proton repulsion |
| Asymmetry | aₐ | 23.70 | N≠Z penalty |
| Pairing | δ | ±11.18 | Even-even bonus |

### 3.2 Nuclear Shell Model Corrections

Magic numbers represent closed nuclear shells:

| Particle | Magic Numbers |
|----------|---------------|
| Protons | 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, **114?, 120?, 126** |
| Neutrons | 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126, **184** |

Shell correction: ΔE = +2.0 MeV (magic Z) + 2.5 MeV (magic N)

### 3.3 Relativistic Corrections (Dirac Equation)

For heavy elements, inner electrons move at relativistic speeds:

```
v/c = Zα ≈ Z/137

Relativistic factor: γ = √(1 - Z²α²)

Effects:
  - s, p₁/₂ orbitals contract
  - d, f, g orbitals expand (shielding)
  - Ionization energy increases
  - Atomic radius decreases
```

## 4. G-Block: A New Frontier of Chemistry

### 4.1 What is the G-Block?

The g-block (Z=121-138) represents elements with electrons in 5g orbitals—**never before observed in any element**.

**Quantum numbers:**
```
Angular momentum: l = 4
Orbitals: 2l + 1 = 9
Maximum electrons: 2(2l+1) = 18
Magnetic quantum numbers: mₗ = -4, -3, -2, -1, 0, +1, +2, +3, +4
```

### 4.2 Comparison of Orbital Types

| Orbital | l | Orbitals | Max e⁻ | First Element |
|---------|---|----------|--------|---------------|
| s | 0 | 1 | 2 | H (Z=1) |
| p | 1 | 3 | 6 | B (Z=5) |
| d | 2 | 5 | 10 | Sc (Z=21) |
| f | 3 | 7 | 14 | Ce (Z=58) |
| **g** | **4** | **9** | **18** | **Ubu (Z=121)** |

### 4.3 G-Orbital Properties

**Angular distribution:**
- 4 angular nodes (most complex shape)
- 8-16 lobes depending on mₗ
- Highly directional bonding

**Radial distribution:**
- 0 radial nodes for 5g
- Maximum probability at r ≈ 25a₀/Z (non-relativistic)
- Significant contraction for superheavy elements

### 4.4 The γ-Bond: A New Type of Chemical Bond

G-orbitals enable a new bond type—the **γ-bond** (gamma bond):

```
Bond types by angular momentum:
  σ (Δmₗ=0): s, p, d, f, g
  π (Δmₗ=1): p, d, f, g
  δ (Δmₗ=2): d, f, g
  φ (Δmₗ=3): f, g
  γ (Δmₗ=4): g only ← NEW!
```

**γ-bond characteristics:**
- 4 nodal planes containing bond axis
- 8-fold rotational symmetry
- Only possible between g-block elements
- First predicted in Ubh₂ dimer
